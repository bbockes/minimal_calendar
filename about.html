A Calendar for Thinking

James Clear, author of Atomic Habits, says the most important habit is reflection.

Because the real leverage in life isn’t effort—it’s direction.

Choosing the right thing to focus on can 10x or 100x your results. But that only happens if you regularly step back and ask:

What are you using your time for?

It’s harder than it sounds.

Your time is scattered across calendars, habit trackers, and notes—fragmented into pieces that never quite come together.

So you stay busy. You move forward. But you’re not always sure where it leads.

You only notice when you stop—and realize something’s missing.

Here’s the problem:

Most tools aren’t built for reflection.

Calendars organize your time. Productivity apps optimize your output. AI helps you move faster—but none of them are designed to show you what your life is adding up to.

And if you can’t see it, you can’t reflect on it.
Learn from it. Improve it.

What’s missing is simple:

A clear, continuous view of your life—all in one place. A way to see your year up close, and step back to understand what it’s becoming.

That’s why we built Your Year.

A minimal, linear calendar for your days, habits, and thoughts—seen across an entire year. Not as isolated entries, but as a story.

A place to zoom out and ask:

What did I actually do this month?
Did my time reflect what I said mattered?
What is my life adding up to?

Because reflection is how you course-correct.

It’s how you close the gap between what you value and how you actually spend your time—and how you recognize the progress you’ve made along the way.

This year—and the next, and the one after that—it’s going to pass regardless.

The question is:

Will it pass by default—or by design?
